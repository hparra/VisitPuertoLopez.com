Puerto Lopez is waiting!

Puerto Lopez is a traditional fishing town on the central coast of Ecuador famous for humpback whale watching, the Machalilla National Park, and the poor man's alternative to the Galapagos, Isla de la Plata. There's tons to see, including trails, forests, beaches and reefs. The Puerto Lopez beach is lined with with huts where you can grab a drink, and local & international cuisine is served all day. When you're ready to relax choose among a variety of hotels & hostels. There is something for everyone, so come on down!

What's there to see in Puerto Lopez?

Whales!

Humpback whales are one of the main reasons people come to Puerto Lopez and the coast of Ecuador, but there are also dolphins, orca, turtles, and manta rays!

Isla de la Plata

Known as "Poor Man's Galapagos" this island an hour off the coast is home to nature trails showcasing an incredible magnitude of majestic birds.

Los Frailes Beach Park

A nature trail takes you to lookout points and three distinct, pristine beaches. If you just want to relax, you can take transpiration directly to the main beach.

El Pital Cloud Forest

A series of trails run through cloud forest full of wildlife, including howler & cappuccino monkeys. Go on foot or take a horse.

Agua Blanca

This settlement over the remains of one of the oldest civilizations in South America hosts a museum, a therapeutic sulfur pool, and a number of nature trails, including one into cloud forest of San Sebastian.

El Rocio EcoTrail

El Rocio trail walks through dry forest filled with wonderful flora as it makes its way down to the rocky beach below.
